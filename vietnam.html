About Viet Nam

Vietnam is a country located in Southeast Asia. It is bordered by China to the north, Laos to the northwest, Cambodia to the southwest, and the South China Sea to the east and south. The total land area of Vietnam is approximately 331,212 square kilometers. The capital city of Vietnam is Hanoi, while the largest city is Ho Chi Minh City.

Vietnam is divided into 58 provinces and 5 municipalities, including Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City, Hai Phong, Can Tho, and Da Nang. The country is known for its stunning landscapes, including mountains, beaches, and rice terraces. The Mekong Delta, located in the southern part of Vietnam, is a fertile region known for its rice production.

Vietnam is a socialist republic with a one-party government led by the Communist Party of Vietnam. The country has a president and a prime minister as its highest-ranking officials. Vietnam is divided into three regions: the north, the central, and the south. The economy of Vietnam has grown rapidly in recent years, with a focus on exports and manufacturing. The country has a population of over 97 million people, making it the 15th most populous country in the world. Vietnamese is the official language, and the majority of the population practices Buddhism.
