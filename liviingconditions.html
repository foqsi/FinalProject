Population

The United States has a population of 328,239,523 people. Viet Nam has a population of 96,491,146 people.

United States

An estimated 83% of the U.S. population lives in urban areas. More than three hundred urban areas in the U.S. have populations of above 100,000; New York City has the largest inhabitants with 8.8 million. Los Angeles, being the second largest city has 4 million inhabitants.

The average monthly rent in the U.S. is $1,000USD. The average monthly rent in urban areas can range from $2,000USD $6,000USD.

Viet Nam

Nearly 34% of the population lives in urban spaces. The biggest cities are Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi, but Haiphong and Da Nang are also becoming very attactive to live, with urban populations of 1.075 million and 952,000 respectively.

Housing in Viet Nam is very cheap, and the average monthly rent is around $50USD. In the overcrowded urban cities, the rent is higher at around $100USD.

Climate

Both countries have multiple climates and experience all seasons.

United States

The climate in the United States is highly diverse, ranging from tropical conditions in Florida and Hawaii to arctic conditions in Alaska and across the Rocky Mountains. The United States has four seasons: spring, summer, fall, and winter. The average temperature in the United States is 57 degrees Fahrenheit. The average temperature in the summer is 75 degrees Fahrenheit. The average temperature in the winter is 32 degrees Fahrenheit.

Education

Both countries have a similar school structure and a high literacy rate.

United States

The United States has a free public education system with a literacy rate of 99%. A formal education is twelve years of basic education. The basic education consists of five years of primary education, four years of secondary, and three years of high school.

The United States has a high school graduation rate of 85%; College enrollment rate of 70% with a graduation rate of 40%.

Viet Nam

Education in Viet Nam is a state-run system of public and private schools and universities ran by the Ministry of Education and Training with a literacy rate of 98%. A formal education consists of twelve years of basic education. The basic education consists of five years of primary education, four years of secondary, and three years of high school.

Viet Nam high school graduation rate is 90%. The college enrollment rate in Viet Nam is 40% and graduation rate is 30%.
