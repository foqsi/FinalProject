Information about the capitols

Washington, D.C., USA

Washington, D.C., or simply D.C., is the capital of the United States. Founded after the American Revolution as the seat of government of the newly independent country, Washington was named after George Washington, first President of the United States and Founding Father. Washington is the principal city of the Washington metropolitan area, which has a population of 6,131,977. Commuters from the surrounding Maryland and Virginia suburbs raise the city's population during the workweek. The Washington metropolitan area, of which the District is a part, is the sixth-largest metropolitan statistical area in the country. The centers of all three branches of the federal government of the United States are including the Congress, Supreme Court, and the President are all located in the District. Washington is home to national museums, which are primarily situated on or around the National Mall. The city hosts 176 foreign embassies as well as the headquarters of many international organizations, trade unions, non-profit, lobbying groups, and professional associations such as the Organization of American States. The city is also a major center for international finance and the home of many international banks and large corporations. The Washington Monument, National Mall, and the Washington Monument are among the city's most visited tourist attractions.

Hanoi, Viet Nam

Hanoi is the capital of Vietnam and the country's second largest city by population. It is located on the right bank of the Red River. The city's population in 2016 was estimated at 7.7 million for urban districts and 7.6 million for the metropolitan jurisdiction, making it the most populous city in Vietnam. The population in 2011 was estimated at 6.6 million. The population of the metropolitan jurisdiction was estimated at 7.1 million in 2016. The city covers an area of 3,358.8 square kilometres (1,295.3 sq mi) including the adjacent Red River Delta and West Lake, and had a population of 6.6 million as of 2015. The population density is around 2,000 inhabitants per square kilometre (5,200/sq mi). The city is administered by the People's Committee of Hanoi. The city is also the political, economic, cultural, and educational centre of Vietnam. It is the centre of the Hanoi Capital Region, which includes the neighbouring province of Hanoi.
